Walleye Research & Methodology

Last updated: 2026-03-16

This page explains the live Walleye model in plain English. It shows what is research-backed, what is calibrated, and what we intentionally do not use. It does not publish proprietary tuning details.

How it works

PrimeBite Walleye uses Major windows only. Each Major window gets a 1.0 to 5.0 rating, and the day rating is the average of those Major-window ratings.

The live Walleye score uses four drivers:

  • Major-window base
  • Moon phase ordering
  • Dawn/dusk light timing
  • Conservative seasonality

The live scale is benchmark-anchored so 1.0 means among the worst realistic Walleye conditions, 2.5 means about average, and 5.0 means among the best realistic conditions.

Research-backed vs calibrated

Driver Status Live role Why
Major-window base Research-backed direction + calibrated magnitude Core lift from moon overhead / underfoot Underfoot stays stronger than overhead, matching the Shaw ordering.
Moon phase Research-backed ordering + calibrated interpolation Raises gibbous windows, lowers quarter-phase windows Waxing and waning gibbous stay strongest. Quarter phases stay weakest.
Dawn/dusk light window Research-backed direction + calibrated fade Direct light lift near sunrise and sunset Dusk is strongest, dawn is next, daytime is weakest.
Seasonality Research-backed direction + calibrated monthly values Small seasonal multiplier Spring gets the highest boost, summer dips slightly, and fall gets a small rebound.
Wind speed / precipitation / pressure / cloud cover Excluded Not used in the live Walleye score They did not survive the full recalibration evidence review.
Separate overlap bonus Excluded Not used in the live Walleye score The direct dawn/dusk light driver replaced the older overlap story.

Evidence anchors

Moon overhead / underfoot

Shaw et al. 2021 reported that anglers were about 10-12% more likely to be successful when the moon was overhead or underfoot.

Moon phase

The same paper reported the highest odds during waxing and waning gibbous phases and the lowest odds during quarter phases.

Dawn / dusk light

Shaw reported dusk strongest, dawn next, and day weakest. Vasquez 2024 provided the practical sunrise/sunset +/- 30 minute definition used for the live light window.

What the scale means

  • 1.0 = among the worst realistic Walleye conditions
  • 2.5 = about average
  • 5.0 = among the best realistic Walleye conditions

In the full 2026 benchmark sweep, the live Walleye model produced:

  • median = 2.50
  • tail <= 1.1 = 1.91%
  • tail >= 4.9 = 1.25%
